The Perl 6 Summary for the week ending 20031116

Evening all. Are you sitting comfortably? Then I'll begin.

Undoubtedly, over the last week, some or all of the following happened:

  • Dan Sugalski thought about design, and maybe even nailed a few more things down.
  • Leo Tötsch wrote far more code than you'd think possible.
  • Luke Palmer either answered or asked a tough question or two on perl6-language.
  • A series of unfortunate events, involving a laptop failing twice in short order and an external firewire drive that decided that 'Write once, read once' was a sensible operating mode, and a general lack of an adequate backup regime, mean that I have lost my mail archive, my summary archive, the tools I use to publish them and, most devastatingly, a week's worth of new mail that I'd not got 'round to reading or replying to yet. Which means I'm not really in a position to write the usual summary this week. I could head off to Google, wrestle with an interface that really isn't suited to reading mail and knock something together that wasn't too awful, but I'm going to punt instead. Hopefully I'll have things vaguely sane next week and I'll see about doing a double summary.

Acknowledgements, Apologies, Announcements
